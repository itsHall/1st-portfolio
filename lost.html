LOST

Publication Proposal

LOST: The Impact of War is a proposal for a larger publication covering topics like the impact of war on the environment, families, mental health, and culture.

INTERACTIVE VIEW

PHILOSOPHY

This publication’s purpose is to inform the reader of the total impact of war. In today’s safer society we often forget the hardships our ancestors faced. Many soldiers, even young men (below 21) fought in some of the most violent conflicts in history. The impacts these conflicts had on the soldiers’ health (mental and physical) and families are unavoidable. The actions of militaries’ and resulting loss of art, architecture, and documents is irreversible. The environmental affects of war is forever scarring. This publication is about things that can never be reclaimed: they should always be remembered, but never repeated.

PROCESS

This publication was designed from the front to the back: beginning with the Table of Contents, then the Feature and finishing with the cover. I sketched out each part of my process to be able to visualize layout, size, and provide more thought about the piece. I chose the subject of the publication because I've been amazed by war and it's aftermath for as long as I can remember. Being such a heavy subject, I knew this publication needed to be respectful and informative. I found all of the content and arranged/designed in Adobe InDesign.
